Must Know - Vital Signs
  • Fever = temp >=38.0C rectal | >=37.5C axilla | Tachycardia ~10 bpm per 1C rise
  • Hypotension = late sign in children - if BP drops = decompensated shock already
  • Fever in infant <3 months >=38C = Serious bacterial infection until proven otherwise - Full sepsis workup
  • SpO2 <95% in room air = abnormal - evaluate immediately
  • UO target: >=1 ml/kg/hr (infant), >=0.5 ml/kg/hr (older child)
Good to Know - Vital Pearls
  • Sinus arrhythmia in children is NORMAL - HR increases on inspiration, decreases on expiration
  • BP cuff size matters: too small = falsely high, too large = falsely low (cuff width = 40% arm circumference)
  • Bradycardia in apneic infant = respiratory cause until proven otherwise - BVM first
  • Orthostatic hypotension: SBP drop >=20 mmHg or DBP drop >=10 mmHg on position change
Ref: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics, 21st Ed.

Must Know - Red Flags พัฒนาการ
  • ไม่ยิ้มตอบสนอง ที่ 2 เดือน = red flag ทันที
  • ไม่ babble เลยที่ 12 เดือน = ประเมิน autism / hearing loss
  • ไม่มี 2-word phrase ที่ 24 เดือน = refer ทันที
  • Regression (เสียทักษะที่มีอยู่) = red flag เสมอ - workup underlying disease
  • ไม่เดิน ที่ 18 เดือน = ประเมิน motor delay, muscular dystrophy, cerebral palsy
Good to Know - Dev Pearls
  • Premature infant: ใช้ corrected age จนถึง 2 ปี (เช่น 6 mo born 2 mo early = correct to 4 mo)
  • Object permanence เริ่ม 8-9 เดือน = peek-a-boo test ได้
  • MCHAT-R ใช้ screen autism ที่ 18 และ 24 เดือน ตาม AAP
  • Denver II = standard screening tool ในไทย ใช้ได้ตั้งแต่แรกเกิด-6 ปี

IV Fluid Calculator S2
คำนวณสารน้ำ (Holliday-Segar)
Must Know - Holliday-Segar Formula
  • 1-10 kg: 100 ml/kg/day
  • 11-20 kg: 1,000 ml + 50 ml/kg (for each kg 11-20)
  • >20 kg: 1,500 ml + 20 ml/kg (for each kg >20)
  • ml/hr = ml/day divided by 24

Good to Know - Practical Tips
  • Moderate dehydration: give 50% deficit in 8h then remaining 50% in 16h
  • Severe/Shock: resuscitate with NSS 20 ml/kg bolus first, then calculate deficit
  • Add KCl 20 mEq/L only after UO returns - NEVER give K if anuric
  • Ongoing loss (diarrhea): replace 10 ml/kg per episode
  • Insensible loss increases ~12% per 1C above 37.5C
Pitfall - Fluid
  • Hypotonic fluid (1/3 NSS) increases risk of hospital-acquired hyponatremia - current guidelines recommend 1/2 NSS or isotonic instead
  • Hypernatremic dehydration: rehydrate slowly - reduce Na no faster than 10-12 mEq/L/day to prevent cerebral edema
  • Do not give IV fluids unnecessarily - ORT is better for mild-moderate dehydration
  • Reduce maintenance 20-30% in SIADH risk (meningitis, pneumonia)
Ref: Harriet Lane Handbook, 22nd Ed. / AAP Clinical Report on Maintenance IV Fluids
ชนิด IV Fluid และข้อบ่งชี้
NSS (0.9% NaCl)

Resuscitation bolus, Dengue fluid challenge | Na: 154 mEq/L | Osmol: 308

Lactated Ringer's (LRS)

Resuscitation, post-op, burns - more physiologic | Na 130, K 4, Ca 3, Lactate 28 | Osmol: ~273

5%D/N/2 (Half-NSS + Dextrose)

Maintenance in infant-child >=28 days | Reduces hyponatremia risk vs 1/3 NSS | Na: 77 mEq/L

5%D/NSS

Maintenance in adolescents, post-op | Na: 154 mEq/L | Watch for hypernatremia if prolonged

KCl Supplement

20-40 mEq/L | Max IV rate 0.3-0.5 mEq/kg/hr | NEVER IV push | Check serum K first always

Must Know - Fluid Selection Rules
  • Shock / Resuscitation: NSS or LRS only - NO dextrose-containing fluids
  • Hypoglycemia: D10W 2-4 ml/kg bolus first
  • NEVER give glucose-containing solution in Head injury / Drowning / Hypoxia - worsens outcome
  • DKA initial fluid: NSS 10-20 ml/kg in 1-2h, then switch to 1/2 NSS + K
Ref: Harriet Lane Handbook, 22nd Ed.

Emergency & Resuscitation S4
PALS Quick Calculator
Must Know - ABCDE Priorities
  • Airway - Open, suction, OPA/NPA adjunct
  • Breathing - BVM 100% O2, intubation if needed
  • Circulation - Compressions + IV/IO access + Fluids/Drugs
  • Disability - GCS, glucose (check BSL immediately), pupils
  • Exposure - Temperature, rash, trauma, bleeding
  • Pediatric cardiac arrest is mostly RESPIRATORY - prioritize airway/breathing first

Must Know - CPR Critical Points
  • Shockable: VF, Pulseless VT - Defib 2 J/kg - CPR 2 min - repeat 4 J/kg
  • Non-shockable: Asystole, PEA - CPR + Epinephrine q3-5 min + treat H's & T's
  • H's: Hypoxia, Hypovolemia, Hypo/Hyperkalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia
  • T's: Tension PTX, Tamponade, Toxins, Thrombosis
  • ETCO2 >40 mmHg during CPR = likely ROSC | <10 mmHg = poor CPR quality
Good to Know - Advanced Resuscitation
  • IO access: if IV fails in 90 sec - go IO immediately | Proximal tibia is 1st choice in children
  • Post-ROSC: SpO2 94-99% (avoid hyperoxia), ETCO2 35-45 mmHg, MAP >=65 mmHg
  • Vasopressin NOT recommended in pediatric arrest (2020 PALS)
  • Capnography: ETCO2 >=10-15 mmHg = adequate CPR quality
Ref: AHA PALS Guidelines 2020

Must Know - Intubation Checklist
  • Prepare ETT +/- 0.5 size (3 sizes) + stylet + suction + BVM always
  • Preoxygenate with 100% O2 BVM for >=3 min before intubation
  • RSI: Atropine 0.02 mg/kg (if Succinylcholine in <1 yr) + Ketamine 1-2 mg/kg + Rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg
  • Confirm ETT placement: bilateral chest rise + ETCO2 + CXR
Good to Know - Airway Tips
  • Infant larynx is higher (C3-C4) + narrow floppy epiglottis - use straight blade (Miller)
  • Ketamine = preferred induction in asthma + hemodynamically unstable (bronchodilator + sympathomimetic)
  • 3-3-2 rule: mouth opening <3F, hyoid-chin <3F, thyroid-floor <2F = difficult airway
Ref: AHA PALS 2020
Pediatric Shock Recognition
Must Know - Shock Types
  • Compensated: HR up, CRT up, cool/pale - BP still NORMAL - do NOT miss!
  • Decompensated: BP low, altered consciousness, oliguria - LATE sign in children
  • Hypovolemic: dehydration/blood loss - NSS/LRS bolus
  • Septic: warm (vasodilation) or cold (vasoconstriction) - fluid + antibiotics +/- vasopressor
  • Cardiogenic: hepatomegaly, rales, gallop - careful with fluids - Dobutamine
  • Obstructive: tension PTX, tamponade - decompress FIRST
Pearl - Septic Shock 1-Hour Bundle
  • Blood cultures BEFORE antibiotics (but do NOT delay antibiotics to wait for BC)
  • Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
  • NSS/LRS 20 ml/kg bolus x3 (reassess after each bolus)
  • Lactate >2 mmol/L = tissue hypoperfusion | >=4 = severe sepsis
  • Norepinephrine (warm shock) or Epinephrine (cold shock) if fluid refractory
Ref: Surviving Sepsis Campaign Pediatric Guidelines 2020
Thai CPGs & Guidelines S5
Dengue Infection
Must Know - Dengue Essentials
  • Critical phase = Days 3-5 of fever - peak plasma leakage
  • NEVER give NSAIDs/Aspirin - Paracetamol only for fever
  • Tourniquet test (+): >=10 petechiae/inch squared = dengue highly likely
  • NS1 Ag: best sensitivity on days 1-5 | IgM: positive after day 5
  • Defervescence = critical phase starting - monitor closely 24-48h after fever breaks

1. Dengue Without Warning Signs - can manage as OPD

2. Dengue With Warning Signs - ADMIT always:

  • Severe abdominal pain or tenderness
  • Vomiting >=3x/hr or persistent
  • Fluid accumulation (ascites/pleural effusion)
  • Mucosal bleeding
  • Lethargy/restlessness
  • Liver enlargement >2 cm
  • Hct rising + rapid PLT drop

3. Severe Dengue - ICU: severe plasma leakage+shock, severe bleeding, severe organ impairment

Start: NSS or LRS 5-7 ml/kg/hr x 1-2h

Reassess q1-2h:

  • Improving: reduce rate 5 - 3 - 2 - 1.5 - maintenance
  • No improvement: repeat 5-10 ml/kg/hr x 1h
  • Shock: 10-20 ml/kg bolus + colloid (Dextran 70 or albumin)
Monitor Hct q4-6h | Watch for fluid overload | Hct dropping + persistent tachycardia may = rebleeding, not fluid overload
Ref: CPG Dengue MoPH Thailand 2022
Good to Know - Dengue Pearls
  • Saddle-back fever = fever drops then returns = classic dengue pattern
  • Relative bradycardia (Faget's sign) = HR lower than expected for fever - common in dengue, NOT a bad sign
  • Recovery phase: Hct dropping + bradycardia + convalescent rash = signs of improvement
  • Platelet transfusion: PLT <10,000 (no bleeding) or <20,000 (with bleeding) only - NOT routine
  • Discharge: afebrile >=24h, PLT improving, no warning signs, good oral intake
Ref: CPG Dengue, Ministry of Public Health Thailand 2022

Mild:
  • Salbutamol MDI 2-4 puffs via spacer q20min x3 in 1h
  • O2 target SpO2 >=95% | Observe 1-4h

  • Salbutamol Neb 0.15 mg/kg (min 2.5 mg) q20min x3

  • Prednisolone oral 1-2 mg/kg/day (max 40 mg) x 3-5 days
Severe/Life-threatening:
  • Continuous Salbutamol nebulization + high flow O2
  • IV Methylprednisolone 1-2 mg/kg q6h
  • IV Magnesium Sulfate 25-75 mg/kg (max 2g) over 20 min
  • Prepare ICU, intubation (Ketamine preferred induction in asthma)

Good to Know - Asthma Pearls
  • Ipratropium is only beneficial for first 3 doses - continuing after provides no added benefit
  • IV Magnesium: bronchodilator via Ca channel block - very effective in severe/life-threatening
  • CXR not needed routinely - order if suspect pneumothorax, pneumonia, or first episode not responding
  • Antibiotics NOT indicated in asthma exacerbation unless clear bacterial infection evidence
Pitfall - Asthma
  • Do NOT discharge too early after a single salbutamol dose - effect often wears off in 1-2h
  • Silent chest = worsening, NOT improvement - escalate treatment immediately
  • Sedatives in non-intubated asthma patients can cause respiratory arrest
Ref: Thai Asthma Guideline 2022 / GINA 2023
Acute Gastroenteritis (AGE)
Must Know - AGE Danger Signs
  • Bloody diarrhea + fever = suspect Shigella, Salmonella, EHEC - stool C/S
  • Infant <3 months + diarrhea = ADMIT and evaluate always
  • Projectile/bilious vomiting = R/O obstruction / pyloric stenosis first
  • HUS = EHEC: bloody diarrhea + microangiopathic anemia + thrombocytopenia + AKI
  • NEVER give Loperamide in children <12 years - dangerous

Good to Know - AGE Pearls
  • Ondansetron reduces vomiting and improves ORT success - reduces IV use and admission rate
  • Probiotics (L. rhamnosus GG, S. boulardii) reduce duration ~1 day - moderate evidence
  • Cola/juice/sports drinks are NOT ORS substitutes - high osmolarity, low Na - worsens diarrhea
  • Antibiotics only for: Shigella (Azithromycin), Cholera (Azithromycin), Giardia (Metronidazole)
  • Continue feeding (no NPO) - early feeding improves recovery
Pitfall - AGE
  • Over-hydration with hypotonic fluids = hyponatremic seizure
  • Hypernatremic dehydration: rehydrate slowly, reduce Na <=10-12 mEq/L/day
  • Do NOT diagnose AGE too quickly - rule out surgical cause (intussusception, appendicitis) if bilious vomiting or severe pain

Neonatal Jaundice - Quick Reference
Must Know - Jaundice Rules
  • Jaundice in first 24h = ALWAYS PATHOLOGICAL - check Total/Direct bilirubin immediately
  • Physiological: appears day 2-3, peaks day 4-5, resolves <2 wks (term) / <3 wks (preterm)
  • Direct bilirubin >2 mg/dL or >20% of total = pathological - workup liver disease
  • G6PD deficiency: severe and rapid jaundice - check G6PD screen in all severe cases
  • Phototherapy/Exchange threshold: use Bhutani nomogram based on GA + postnatal age (hours)
Good to Know - Phototherapy Tips
  • Adequate hydration during phototherapy - insensible water loss increases significantly
  • Breast milk jaundice: peaks week 2-3, can last 3-12 weeks - do NOT stop breastfeeding
  • Bronze baby syndrome: cholestatic jaundice + phototherapy - STOP phototherapy immediately
  • After phototherapy: check bilirubin rebound at 12-24h after stopping
Ref: AAP Neonatal Hyperbilirubinemia Guidelines 2022 / Nelson 21st Ed.

การเจริญเติบโต (Growth Monitoring)
Must Know — Growth Rules
  • ใช้ WHO Growth Charts (0-5 yr) และ Thai Growth Reference (5-18 yr)
  • Failure to Thrive: น้ำหนักต่ำกว่า 3rd percentile หรือ weight faltering ข้าม 2 major centile lines
  • น้ำหนักทารกแรกเกิด: ลดได้ไม่เกิน 10% ใน 3-5 วัน — กลับสู่ birth weight ภายใน 10-14 วัน
  • Average weight gain: 0-3 mo = 25-30 g/day | 3-6 mo = 15-20 g/day | 6-12 mo = 10-15 g/day
Good to Know — Quick Estimates
  • Double birth weight ~5 months | Triple ~12 months
  • Estimated weight (2-12 yr): (Age yr x 2) + 8 kg
  • Head circumference: Birth ~34 cm | 6 mo ~43 cm | 12 mo ~46 cm | 2 yr ~48 cm
  • Anterior fontanelle closes: 9-18 months (mean 13 mo) | Posterior: 2-3 months
Ref: Thai Pediatric Society / WHO Child Growth Standards

Must Know — APGAR
  • APGAR ไม่ใช่ตัวกำหนดการ resuscitate — ตัดสินใจ resuscitate ก่อน แล้วค่อยบันทึก APGAR
  • APGAR 5-min <7 = ให้ score ซ้ำทุก 5 นาที จนถึง 20 นาที
  • APGAR 5-min <5 = risk neonatal encephalopathy — พิจารณา therapeutic hypothermia
Ref: AAP/AHA Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP) 8th Ed.
Neonatal Jaundice — Phototherapy Guide
Must Know — Jaundice
  • Jaundice ใน 24h แรก = Pathological เสมอ — ตรวจ TB ทันที
  • Direct bilirubin >2 mg/dL หรือ >20% of total = Pathological — workup cholestasis
  • Kernicterus risk: Hemolysis, G6PD def, Prematurity, Sepsis, Acidosis, Hypoalbuminemia

Good to Know — PT Tips
  • เพิ่ม fluid 10-20% ระหว่าง phototherapy เพราะ IWL เพิ่มขึ้น
  • Breast milk jaundice: peak สัปดาห์ที่ 2-3, นาน 3-12 สัปดาห์ — ไม่ต้องหยุดนมแม่
  • Bronze baby syndrome = cholestasis + PT — หยุด phototherapy ทันที
  • Check bilirubin rebound 12-24h หลังหยุด PT
  • Standard: ใช้ Bhutani nomogram ตาม GA + postnatal hours

Must Know — Critical Findings
  • Red reflex absent = refer ophthalmology stat (congenital cataract / retinoblastoma)
